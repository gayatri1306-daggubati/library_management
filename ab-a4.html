Logical Thinking and Problem-Solving

Theory Explanation:

Logical thinking and problem-solving involve the ability to analyze situations, break them into smaller parts, and apply reasoning to find solutions. These skills are crucial for solving puzzles effectively and efficiently.

Key Concepts:

  • Pattern Recognition: Identifying trends in numbers, shapes, or words.
  • Deductive Reasoning: Drawing conclusions from given statements or premises.
  • Analytical Thinking: Breaking problems into smaller, manageable parts.
  • Creative Problem-Solving: Thinking outside the box to find unique solutions.

MCQs on Logical Thinking and Problem-Solving

1. What is the next number in the sequence? 2, 6, 12, 20, ?, 42

a) 30
b) 28
c) 32
d) 34

Answer: b) 28

2. A farmer has 17 sheep, and all but 9 run away. How many sheep does he have left?

a) 9
b) 8
c) 17
d) 0

Answer: a) 9

3. What comes next in the sequence: 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, ?

a) 20
b) 21
c) 22
d) 24

Answer: b) 21

4. If a clock shows 3:15, what is the angle between the hour and minute hands?

a) 0 degrees
b) 7.5 degrees
c) 30 degrees
d) 45 degrees

Answer: b) 7.5 degrees

5. A man buys a horse for ₹600, sells it for ₹700, buys it back for ₹800, and sells it again for ₹900. What is his total profit?

a) ₹100
b) ₹200
c) ₹300
d) ₹400

Answer: b) ₹200

6. There are 5 apples in a basket. You take away 3. How many do you have?

a) 2
b) 3
c) 5
d) 0

Answer: b) 3

7. How many times does the digit 9 appear between 1 and 100?

a) 10
b) 18
c) 19
d) 20

Answer: c) 19

8. A snail climbs 3 feet during the day and slips down 2 feet at night. How many days will it take to reach the top of a 10-foot pole?

a) 7 days
b) 8 days
c) 9 days
d) 10 days

Answer: a) 7 days

9. What is the next number in the sequence? 4, 9, 16, 25, ?

a) 30
b) 35
c) 36
d) 49

Answer: c) 36

10. A bus can hold 40 passengers. If 10 passengers get off at each stop and 15 new passengers get on, how many passengers will be on the bus after 4 stops?

a) 40
b) 50
c) 60
d) 70

Answer: b) 50
